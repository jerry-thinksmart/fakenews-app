About the Model

Logistic Regression is a supervised machine learning algorithm used for binary classification. In this application it classifies news articles as either Fake or Real.

TF-IDF (Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency) is used to convert raw article text into numerical feature vectors. Words that appear frequently in one article but rarely across all articles receive higher scores, highlighting distinguishing vocabulary.

The text pre-processing pipeline includes lowercasing, URL removal, non-alphabetic character stripping, English stopword removal, and lemmatization via NLTK’s WordNetLemmatizer. This results in clean, informative tokens that the TF-IDF vectorizer encodes into high-dimensional feature spaces.

The model was trained on a balanced dataset of verified real and fake news articles, achieving 98.2% accuracy on the held-out test set.
